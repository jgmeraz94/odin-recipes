Homemade Birria Tacos

Ever craved those delicious street tacos but don't want to get out of the house? Well you're in luck because these Birria tacos are just as good if not better than your favorite taco truck, and the best part is that you will be able to make them whenever you want.

This dish consist of beef stewed in a broth that will soften the meat to where it melts in your mouth. The broth is made with dried chili peppers, an onion, garlic, dried chili peppers, and a blend of seasonings. Down below I will show you all the ingredients that you will need to make this delicious dish.

Ingredients

  • Dried Chili Peppers: Guajillo chile peppers, chile de arbol peppers, and chile ancho peppers.
  • Beef: Chuck roast is perfect for braising for hours, when done the meat will shred very easily.
  • Seasonings: Salt, pepper, garlic cloves, cinnamon, cumin, thyme, marjoram, and oregano.
  • Olive oil: To sear the meat before adding it to the broth.
  • Tomatoes: Grill roma tomatoes before blending them with other ingredients.
  • Vinegar: Two tablespoons of white vinegar adds even more acidity to the broth.
  • Tortillas: Store-bought or homemade corn tortillas.
  • Onion and cilantro: To garnish your tacos to give it an extra kick in flavor as your take your first bite.

How to make

  1. Boil the dried peppers for 5 minutes, then remove from heat and let the peppers soak until the water is cool. Drain, reserving some water.
  2. Season the beef and sear in oil.
  3. Grill the tomatoes until the skin is blistered and begins to peel.
  4. Blend soaked chili peppers with reserved chile water, tomatoes, vinegar, and seasonings.
  5. Strain the sauce and pour over the meat. Cover it all and let it cook until the meat begins to fall apart, basting often. Allow the meat to rest and then shred.
  6. Warm the tortillas on a griddle. Fill each tortilla with meat, onions, and cilantro. Now you're ready to enjoy your homemade Birria tacos!
